Who Picked Nick Gonzales on 2020 MLB Draft? His Family, Dating, Facts

Publish date: 2024-01-29

Nick Gonzales was selected by the Pittsburgh as the seventh overall pick in the 2020 Major League Baseball Draft (MLB). He was ecstatic by the move and thanked the team for making his dreams of playing at the professional level come true.

One of the talented young prospects in the baseball game, Nick Gonzales played as a shortstop and second baseman for the New Mexico State Aggies before booking his place in Major League Baseball.

Insights on Nick Gonzales Bio & Family: Age, Parents, Height, Education

Nick Gonzales, age 21, was born on 27 May 1999 in Vail, Arizona, the United States. He is among the three children of his parents, Mikes Gonzales and Jill Bosland. Nick shares a sibling bond with his older brother named Daniel Gonzalez and a younger sister. His brother, Daniel, was a star linebacker and captain at Navy from 2013 to 2016.

Speaking about his education, the player with the height of 5 feet and 10 inches (1.78 meters) attended Cienega High School in Vail, Arizona. Later, he enrolled at the New Mexico State University and played college baseball there.

His High School & College Career; MLB Draft

During his high school career, Nick hit .399 with 89 runs batted in (RBI). Eventually, he joined New Mexico State University and started playing for the New Mexico State Aggies. During his freshman year in 2018, Nick hit .347/.425/.596 with 9 home runs and 36 RBIs over 57 games. Later as a sophomore in 2019, he led the nation with a .432 batting average. He concluded his season hitting .432/.532/.773 with 16 home runs and 80 RBIs.
