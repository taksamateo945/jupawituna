1. Comedy 

Bert Kreischer’s net worth has been most heavily influenced by his comedic pursuits. As a matter of fact, comedy is his first love and it is his biggest money spinner so far. He is primarily known as a comedian and is adored for his ability to crack ribs around the world. Bert’s journey into the world of stand-up comedy began at Potbelly’s, a lively bar and nightclub nestled in Tallahassee, Florida. Little did he know that this humble start would lead him to become one of the most renowned and beloved comedians in the industry.

According to our findings, one day, Kreischer did very well at Potbelly’s, cracking ribs and attracting attention. After a while, a tape recording of one of his sets was sent to a talent agent and things changed. The talent agent was very impressed so he invited Kreischer to New York City. Kreischer immediately packed his bags and headed to the bustling streets of New York. There, he honed his craft and got his foot in the door of the comedy scene by working at the now-defunct Boston Comedy Club.

Bert Kreischer quickly made a name for himself with his unique and daring performance style, often taking to the stage while shirtless, which became one of his signature trademarks. But it wasn’t just his appearance that set him apart; it was his incredible storytelling abilities that truly won the hearts of his audience.

One of his most legendary and widely celebrated tales revolves around an unforgettable incident in Russia, where he accidentally found himself embroiled with the Russian mafia during a college trip. This unforgettable story earned him the nickname “The Machine” and has become a cornerstone of his comedic repertoire.

Kreischer’s comedic talent soon caught the attention of larger audiences, leading to appearances on prominent late-night talk shows such as The Late Show with David Letterman, Jimmy Kimmel Live, and Conan. His infectious humor and captivating storytelling also landed him a recurring guest spot on Rachael Ray’s show from 2011 to 2015.

In 2004, he was featured on the DVD release National Lampoon Live: New Faces – Volume 2, alongside four other talented comedians. Additionally, one of his engaging stories was adapted into an episode of the animated Comedy Central series Shorties Watchin’ Shorties, showcasing the breadth of his comedic prowess.

Kreischer’s rise to stardom continued with his debut comedy special, Bert Kreischer: Comfortably Dumb, which aired on Comedy Central in 2009. This marked the beginning of a fruitful relationship with the network, as he returned in 2015 for the storytelling series This Is Not Happening, where he recounted his extraordinary experience of wrestling a bear. Branching out further, Showtime released Bert Kreischer: The Machine in 2016, solidifying his presence in the comedy world.

However, it was his collaboration with Netflix that truly elevated his status as a comedy heavyweight. The streaming giant produced three of his uproarious comedy specials, including Bert Kreischer: Secret Time in 2018, Bert Kreischer: Hey Big Boy in 2020, and Bert Kreischer: Razzle Dazzle in 2023.

His collaboration with Netflix is reported to have fetched Bert Kreischer a lot of money.
