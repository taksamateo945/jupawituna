Danny Masterson Career:

In a season one episode of “Jake and the Fatman” in 1988, Danny Masterson had his first chance to play a character on television: a young boy named Butch. He made his acting debut in the family film “Beethoven’s 2nd” in 1993. He was also chosen to play Leo Gennaro in the short-lived ABC sitcom “Joe’s Life” that same year. He appeared in a number of recurring parts in the ABC dramas “NYPD Blue,” “Extreme,” and the police procedural drama “Roseanne” between 1994 and 1995.

Masterson made several movie appearances in the 1990s, including “Bye Bye Love,” “Face/Off,” “Trojan War,” “Star Kid,” “The Faculty,” and “Dirt Merchant.” He made cameo appearances in the 1996 seasons of the CBS horror series “American Gothic,” the HBO sketch comedy “Tracey Takes On,” and the Fox adolescent drama “Part of Five.”

In 1996, he appeared in his debut television movie, “Seduced by Madness.” He portrayed Justin Thorpe in Chuck Lorre’s “Cybill” from 1996 to 1998. Masterson started acting in a supporting capacity on the television series “That ’70s Show” in 1998. Ashton Kutcher played Michael Kelso, who was a friend of Masterson’s character and belonged to the same group of friends in the same show.

He portrayed Nightshade in the vampire horror movie Dracula 2000. He was selected for the black comedy “Comic Book Villains” two years later. In the 2000s and 2010s, he continued to work on low- to mid-budget movies.

Masterson debuted as the lead in the TBS sitcom “Men at Work” in 2012. He played Milo Foster, a character trying to break into the dating scene with the aid of his pals after just ending a relationship. Breckin Meyer, Michael Cassidy, Adam Busch, Meredith Hagner, and James Lesure all appeared in the TV show in addition to him. TBS showed the program for three years until being canceled in May 2014.

In the Netflix comedy “The Ranch,” Masterson and Kutcher reunited to play the brothers Colt (Kutcher) and Jameson “Rooster” Bennett. Beau Bennett, the father of Colt and Rooster, is portrayed on the program by Sam Elliott, and Maggie Bennett, their mother, is portrayed by Debra Winger.

On “Men at Work” and “The Ranch,” Masterson served as a producer. Additionally, he produced the 2008 movie “The Brooklyn Heist,” for which he shared the Jury Award for Best Florida Comedy.

Danny Masterson has been working as a disc jockey in the nightlife scene in Los Angeles since 1999. Since then, it has evolved into a side venture for him. He frequently calls himself a “rock and hip-hop aficionado.” He also enjoys indie, electro, and funk music, which he incorporates into his DJ performances.

In Park City, Utah, a nightclub and lounge called Downstairs once belonged to Masterson. He enjoyed playing poker and took part in The James Woods Gang vs. The Unabombers on GSN in 2005 as the last team member.

Masterson is a Scientologist, and some people have criticized him for his association with the contentious charity group New York Rescue Workers Detoxification Project. The group is financially linked to the Church of Scientology.

After casting seven ballots over the previous two days, the jury foreman informed the court that they were still divided on each accusation.
