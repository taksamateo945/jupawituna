Dennis Quaid's Epic Meltdown Finally Explained in Funny or Die Sketch

Publish date: 2024-07-05

Dennis Quaid is here! The king of pranks — lest we all forget his on-point Ellen DeGeneres gaffes — has pulled off another one. On Monday, April 13, a video “leaked” online of the Truth actor blowing up on an undisclosed set. 

Fans immediately pointed the finger at Jimmy Kimmel and Ellen DeGeneres, unwilling to believe that Quaid, 61, would ever get that apoplectic (and potty-mouthed!) on set. It turns out they were right, but didn’t place their blame in the right spot. 

On Wednesday, April 15, Funny or Die released a new clip, mocking the headlines the original video made. In the sketch, you see Quaid shouting, “This is horses—t!” as he steps in actual horse feces. 

All of his insults are fully explained from the “d—head” comments to the “f—king baby” bash. 

Shortly after the video went live, David Spade posted an Instagram of Quaid putting Seth Green in a chokehold, writing, “This Dennis Quaid video is no joke. He killed @sethgreen today. @funnyordie.”
